Industry Engagement

To boost my awareness of the cybersecurity industry today and to hear industry experts' perspectives on future advancements in the field, I attended some webinars and increased my knowledge of my industry.


Cyber-Security challenges and future perspectives


Event Page

In this webinar, I gained insight about the importance of cybersecurity in the current landscape, and learned about the different types of projects and measures being taken by companies to inculcate cybersecurity awareness in their employees. The webinar was hosted by projects Cyber-MAR and FORESIGHT, two EU-based projects focused on developing cyber awareness in the maritime sector (Cyber-MAR), aviation, power grid and naval sectors (FORESIGHT). I have a personal interest in the maritime and aviation sectors, so I was interested to find out more about cybersecurity in those sectors, the types of attacks prevalent in those industries today and the actions being taken to combat them. Moreover, with Singapore's recent strategies strengthening cybersecurity in our maritime and aviation sectors, I wanted to find out if there was anything else that Singapore could do in the future to protect our these sectors and our economy better.


Trailblazing the Artificial Intelligence for Cybersecurity Discipline


Event Page

I attended this webinar because though I am a cybersecurity student, I have also studied deep learning, and it was interesting to know how the two disciplines could merge. In the webinar, it was highlighted that a lot of cybersecurity tasks are repetitive, which is why there is room for AI in cybersecurity, to take over the repetitive tasks so that efforts can be focused on other tasks. AI is also being used to boost analysis, in measures that were discussed in the webinar. The webinar also covered possible ways to cultivate AI for the cybersecurity discipline in the future, and my key takeaway from this experience is to continue to upskill and familiarise myself with new technology. This is because there are always new technologies being developed that can boost efficiency and accuracy even in tasks in my own field.


Service Learning

Service-Learning provided me with the opportunity to apply what I learnt in school to help my community. It also taught me values that come in useful for my everyday life and for when I enter the workforce. In November 2021, I conducted a virtual Service-Learning project with my classmates in conjunction with Yong-en Care Center to teach elderly how to use Canva.

In this project, I was an English teacher, showing and explaining to the beneficiaries the functionalities of the software and guiding them through it. My tasks included guiding them through the steps to create designs on Canva through Zoom, with the help of my fellow classmates (who were teachers with me), making sure everyone understood what was being taught and helping the beneficiaries with any issues they faced. Before the actual event, we also had to create the lesson material, which involved a lot of planning and time management.

We faced many issues on the day itself, some main issues being the difficulty in guiding some beneficiaries who were lost, last-minute changes to the schedule due to time constraints, and sudden changes in the system which caused me to take over a section which I had not prepared to teach.

Despite the issues, the event was deemed a successful one as the beneficiaries were satisfied and had learnt something new at the end of the session, and me and my classmates did too. Personally, I have taken away from this the understanding that it is important to be aware and considerate of the people I am working with, and to be able to adapt to new situations. I also developed my personal values of team management and patience.

Isha Jethmal, 2021.
